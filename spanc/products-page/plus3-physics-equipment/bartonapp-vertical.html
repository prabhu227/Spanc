Determination of Rigidity Modulus by Static Method (Vertical Type)

Model Number: SPML-011V

Technical Specifications

  • A steel frame mounted on a heavy cast iron base with levelling screws
  • Upper end of the test rod (900 mm length, 5 mm diameter) clamped by a 3-jaw chuck
  • Lower end clamped into the axis of a torsional drum, which can be rotated by placing load in the scale pan passing over two frictionless pulleys
  • Three freely sliding circular scales graduated in single degrees, which can be clamped at any position
  • Three pointers for clamping to the test rod
  • Complete with three test rods, strings, and two scale pans

Experiment

  • To determine the rigidity modulus by the static method

About the Experiment

The rigidity modulus of a rod can be determined using the static method. In this setup, the test rod is clamped at one end and subjected to rotational motion via a torsional drum. The torque applied helps in calculating the rigidity modulus of the material.
