Introduction

Barium Titanate (BaTiO₃) is a well-known and widely investigated dielectric material, primarily used in capacitors due to its high dielectric constant. The dielectric properties of BaTiO₃ are controlled by purity and microstructure, which are dependent on preparation methods. Its ability to store energy is attributed to polarization, which results in an increase in capacitance. Dielectric properties are highly sensitive to the electric field distribution, temperature, and frequency.
