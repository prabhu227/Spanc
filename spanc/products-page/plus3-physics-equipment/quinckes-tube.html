Quinck's Tube Method

Experimental setup for measuring susceptibility of a paramagnetic solution using Quinck’s Tube.

Technical Specifications

  • Electromagnet: Soft iron, 50mm dia. tapered pole pieces, field: 7.5kg at 10mm air gap. Coils: 2 × 9 ohm (approx.).
  • Digital Power Supply: 0–6A, 60V with digital display. Constant current, long-duration use.
  • Digital Gauss Meter: 0–2K & 0–20K Gauss ranges. Resolution: 1 Gauss. Accuracy: ±0.5%. LED display. 220V, 50Hz.
  • Quinck’s Tube with Stand: Graduated glass tube with sturdy wooden stand.
  • Sample: FeCl₃ solution (Paramagnetic).
  • Travelling Microscope: Cast iron base, 10× eyepiece, 50–75mm working distance, precision vernier scales (0.01mm), leveling screws.

Experiment

Measurement of magnetic susceptibility of a paramagnetic solution using Quinck’s Tube. The solution rises or falls in a magnetic field, and its displacement is measured using a travelling microscope.
