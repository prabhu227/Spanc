Quinck's Tube Method

This image shows the experimental setup for the Quinck's Tube Method to measure the susceptibility of a paramagnetic solution.

Technical Specifications

  • ELECTROMAGNET: Made of soft iron, mounted on a wooden base, pole pieces: 50mm dia. tapered type. Field: 7.5kg at 10mm air gap. Energizing Coils: Two coils each with total resistance of 9 ohms (approx.).
  • DIGITAL POWER SUPPLY FOR ELECTROMAGNET: Range 0-6Amp, 60V digital display, constant current power supply with long-time operation and continuously variable current.
  • DIGITAL GAUSS METER: Range 0-2 K Gauss & 0-20 K Gauss. Resolution: 1 Gauss at 0-2 K Gauss Range. Accuracy: ± 0.5%. Display: 3 1⁄2 Digit, 7 Segment LED. Power: 220V, 50Hz. Special Feature: Indicates the direction of the magnetic field.
  • QUINCK'S TUBE WITH STAND: A graduated glass tube with wooden stand.
  • SAMPLE: FeCl₃ Solution.
  • TRAVELLING MICROSCOPE: Consists of cast iron base with machined V-shape vertical pillar, microscope tube with 10x eyepiece and an achromatic objective of 50-75 mm working distance. Embedded stainless steel horizontal scale graduated up to 17 cm and vertical scale 15 cm. Vertical and horizontal slide can be clamped at any position using clamping screw. Fine motion via micrometer screw. Vernier scales for horizontal and vertical motion (0.01 mm precision). Two leveling screws included.
