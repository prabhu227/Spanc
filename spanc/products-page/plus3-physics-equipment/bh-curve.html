Theory

Hysteresis refers to the lagging of the magnetization of a ferromagnetic material like iron. When magnetized in one direction, the material does not return to zero magnetization immediately after the field is removed. A reverse field is needed to demagnetize it, forming the BH curve or hysteresis loop. Applying an alternating magnetic field results in a closed loop on the B-H graph, indicating energy loss per cycle.
