Plant Physiology

Explore the fascinating inner workings of plants — from photosynthesis to nutrient transport.

Plant Physiology is the study of vital functions and processes in plants. This includes understanding how plants grow, reproduce, and respond to their environment. Core topics include:

  • Photosynthesis – how plants convert sunlight into energy
  • Respiration – the breakdown of food to release energy
  • Water and nutrient transport through xylem and phloem
  • Hormonal regulation of growth and development
  • Environmental responses like phototropism and geotropism

This field is essential for understanding agriculture, ecology, and biotechnology. Hands-on experiments in plant physiology help students grasp biological mechanisms and their practical applications in real-world farming and plant science.
